ABOUT

At TU Dresden, I delved into residential architecture as a creative process—designing human-centered spaces that respond to how we live, interact, and feel at home. Grounded in a socially conscious approach, I’m now driven to integrate innovative and sustainable strategies into my future work.

For me, luxury is about thoughtful, human-centered design that fosters comfort and quality without relying on resource-heavy materials. This approach enhances individual living by strengthening community ties and countering the individualism of modern Western life, while promoting sustainability and responsible building practices.

An academic year at the Polytechnic of Milan broadened my understanding of interior and urban design. This was further enriched by an internship at Casatibuonsante architects in Milan, where I honed a meticulous attention to detail in space development—skills that complemented my prior research into communal living practices and typologies. The urban design exposure, in particular, expanded my perspective, prompting me to consider wider contextual factors.

This synthesis of detailed design acumen and contextual awareness drives my aim: to create spaces that seamlessly integrate into their surroundings, offering an aspirational yet responsible way of living that resonates on both micro and macro scales.
